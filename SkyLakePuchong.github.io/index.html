FAQ

 It's Leasehold. Will it be less valuable?

First, there’s anecdotal evidence that values of both freehold and leasehold properties appreciate at a similar rate for the first 20+ years.

Second, you should realize that even when you own a freehold land, you own it in perpetuity, the state government has the powers to forcibly acquire it from you. Therefore, the idea of “owning a piece of land forever” is somewhat fallible. Nothing is really forever.

Third, do you know that a building structure by Malaysian standards can only last for 60 years?

Condo or flats that were built around 1950-an had all been demolished and rebuilt already. Currently condominiums in Klang Valley are mostly built around 2000 until now (2017). 
If a building structure is of bad quality and meant to be taken down for reconstruction after 50 years.

So, whether a property is freehold or leasehold should be secondary compared to other factors like location, developer reputation, concept, and upside opportunity.

 I buy for investment. What are the upsides?

Good thing. SkyLake Residence is located at the border between Puchong and Cyberjaya. On one side, Puchong is a mature area, on the other side, Cyberjaya is a tech park with residences that nearly 100,000 people call home. With plenty government plans to 'reboot' Cyberjaya, it's pretty much underrated at the moment.

Isn't it too far?

How far is far? Talking about location, it is relative. For example, Desa ParkCity located at the border of Kuala Lumpur’s Kepong and Selangor’s Petaling Jaya. Do you know that it is located at the farthest point of Kepong town centre and Petaling Jaya? It is also about 20km away from KL city centre. 10 years ago nobody believe in its township concept that were built on wasteland. Today it's one of the most premium residence in Klang Valley. And 10 years later, the best second chance will be investing in high growth area with full of potentials, like Puchong border or Cyberjaya, Nilai, or even Semenyih. Learn more.

 Can I afford with monthly income of RM3000?
